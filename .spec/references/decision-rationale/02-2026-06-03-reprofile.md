The impartial profiler ran in **Full mode on 2026-06-03**, emitting per-category rankings to
`src/routing-table.json` (+ per-pairing citations `src/routing-table-audit.json`). The seed/CR tables
in the parent no longer assert "pending"; the route spine in `assets/` stays frozen (see
`../routing-table.md`).

**Approach — deterministic builder (no LLM in the ranking path).** Scores come from a deterministic
pipeline over `structured-dataset.json` (7 models, 22 pairings, 301 benchmark rows): per-benchmark
min-max normalization, equal-weight composite per category, then dense best→worst ranking. Design of
record: `giga-research/2026-06-03-profiling/builder-design.md`. This honors "code answers routing;
model only for judgment" — no model call decides a rank.

**The 3 owner SOPs applied** (encoded in `skills/model-profiler/references/tier-ranking-and-scoring/01-sops.md`):
- **SOP-1 version-promotion** — a missing lineage version inherits its nearest strictly-older listed
  predecessor's score, inserted immediately above it; guard: no older listed version → no insert (falls
  to data-free sentinel). Drove the opus-4-8 promotions above opus-4-7 in data-thin categories.
- **SOP-2 worst-case cost** — tokenizer inflation = documented **1.35× max** (1.4× deprecated, §CR-8);
  gpt-5.5 100K-in blend is provably sub-cliff (272K threshold) → base rate, `price_cliff_side:"below"`.
- **SOP-3 sourcing** — thin (vendor-only/single-press) evidence may move a pairing ≤±1 tier and only on
  a ≥0.10 normalized gap; withdrawn vendor self-reports discarded (gpt-5.5 SWE-bench Verified rows on
  coding+debugging never normalized/cited); tier-2/3 corroboration lifts the cap.

**Calibration gate — PASSED.** `metadata.calibrated_exponents{a=1, b=0.5}` (a>b perf bias);
`cost_efficiency = perf^a / cost^b`. Floors `k_categories_min=3, m_rank_churn_min=3`; observed
**k=8, m=9** (8 categories show ≥3-position perf↔cost rank churn; max churn 9). Cheapest-AND-weakest
ban satisfied structurally by the perf bias.

## RESIDUAL RISKS (verbatim — flag for future research backfill)

1. **Severe data sparsity.** Most of the 22×10 cells have no exact-effort measured row. `mechanical`
   has **zero universe-model benchmark rows → every pairing is a data-free sentinel** (order is
   tie-break only, not measured). `quality_review` has **zero exact-effort rows → low confidence**
   (and an extreme polarity split: gpt-5.5 1.0 vs opus-4-8 0.0 off the 2 inverted benchmarks).
2. **Benchmark-polarity assumption.** All benchmarks assumed **higher-is-better EXCEPT the 2 inverted
   bug-density / false-report benchmarks** (lower-is-better → inverted). Dataset gives no polarity flag;
   builder assumption, not verified per-source.
3. **effort='any' tie compression.** Admitting model-level `any`-effort rows as evidence for every
   effort of that model makes sibling efforts share scores → ties → tie-break ordering carries weight;
   reduces intra-model effort discrimination.
4. **gpt-5.5 / gpt-5.5-pro share `version_rank`** (both 55) → **no cross-promotion** (SOP-1 needs
   strictly-older); treated as distinct siblings, never auto-promoted across each other.

These are flagged for a **future research backfill** (mechanical + quality_review benchmark rows;
per-source polarity confirmation; exact-effort coverage).
